DRAGON BALL Z AUDIO REMAKE

This mixdown was made for my Sound Design 2 class. The project outline was to take an existing animation, take the sound completely out, and rebuild it back into the scene. I chose to remake a Dragon Ball Z fight scene, because I love the show, and I knew it would give me a great challenge for this project. Luckily, I found a soundpack for Dragon Ball Z sounds, which came directly from the show! There are a LOT of impacts in this scene, meaning I had to line up each punching/kicking/moving sound effect with the animation. It took me a lot of time, but I'm very proud of how the final project turned out! I also composed, mixed, and mastered the song that you hear in the background. I wanted to go for a song that fits the theme of Dragon Ball Z, so I made a high-intensity rock song to help drive the action. Overall, this project was a blast, and I am very happy with the finished mixdown!

AUDIO STORY

In my Sound Design 2 class, we learned a lot of techniques on how to tell a story by just using audio, and in this project, I put my skills to use! The outline of this project was to tell a complete, coherent story using only sound effects and a music score. My idea I went with was to tell a story about a survivor of a nuclear blast. I went with the second-person approach, having the pont-of-view from a mute protagonist who meets a talkative, almost too happy-go-lucky survivor in the end times. At first, I wanted to make the whole thing a lot more dark and depressing, but I decided to add a little comedy to make it not as heavy. However, there is a flashback when the man starts to tell his story, and I tried to convey this flashback just through sound. The score behind this part was composed and mixed by me, in order to get it to fit perfectly within the time. I think this project turned out great, and voice acting is something that I probably won't ever do again, haha!
